Geometry Formulas

- Formula for phythagoras theorum ( used in a right angle triangle )
a2+ b2= c2
a being one side of a triangle
b being one side of a triangle
c being the longest side of a triangle ( hypotenuse {c2})
- Sum of all the angles in a triangle ( iscoceles, scalene and equilateral triangle ) =180 degrees

Phythagoras Theorum

In a right triangle ABC, AB is equal to 2cm (a), BC is equal to 2cm (b), find AC(c)?
ans- a2+ b2= c2
22+ 22= c2
4 + 4 = c2
8 = c2
c = 4


Sum of all angles

In a triangle, there is one angle (a) that is equal to 50 degrees, another angle (b) that is 60 degrees, Find the third angle(c)?
ans- 50 degrees + 60 degrees + c = 180 degrees ( angle sum property of triangle )
110 degrees + c = 180 degrees
c = 180 degrees - 110 degrees
c = 70 degrees
